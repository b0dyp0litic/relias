Control released in 2019 and became a masterclass in the New Weird. It was boundary pushing on narrativization, technology, sound and music integration, genre-bending, and flirtations with fourth wall breaking. The world of Control is in a massive windowless skyscraper in lower Manhattan that houses a governmental department called the Federal Bureau of Control, designed after the telecommunications hub and NSA building on 33 Thomas Street in Tribeca. You play as Jesse Fayden, given virtually no introduction, you enter the building discover the director of the Bureau has been killed and you are now the director, for some reason. From here out is a strange journey through the Bureau, through its Bureaucracy, through its remaining staff, and through its external implications. The story isn’t the focus here, how Contorl represents the new weird and by extension speaks to the alienation of being alive in a time where systems architecture, algorithms, and non-human intelligences conflict with our existence but are pushed off into the corners of our mind as somehow just business as usual, is what this is about.
