I wanted to continue on with the theme of the outside entity, the alien being, invading, or injecting itself into a space of assumed reference but showing extreme alterations on pieces of my memory from the condition they are currently in. I grew up on a piece of farmland that was converted into a small nine hole golf course. At times of year like now, that being February, this land was largely abandoned. It was mostly grass and forest in any direction you would look. As this area of Pennsylvania became, essentially, a suburb of New York City, more and more infrastructure built closer and closer through the woods and slowly disintegrated entire parts of the tree-line. Capital, growth, has an all-consumptive mechanism to it. It is assimilative and it isn’t particularly concerned for what it consumes or what that once may have represented as it moves in it’s own self-interest.
