I struggled with the patterns at first. I have done plenty of visual owrk over the years but aside from making a single gift for somebody I don't think I've done pattern work since I was a teenager. That is when it hit me. Why not just replocate the kind of pattern work I did when I was young. In the ninties I used to make texture pack mods for DOS and early Windows based games like Wolfenstein 3D and Doom, or I'd use RPG makers and things and do tile editing. I decided to build out a grid system to color pixels into patterns but leave conventioal expectations of how pixel graphic bricklaying should look. The first pattern was an analogous color relationship just pushed into high contrast to seem completely unmatchable to real world strcuctures that wouldnt be completely plastic. Intentionally leaning toward NES vibes. The second is a split-complimentary color choices and is intended more toward Win 3.1 background tiling.
