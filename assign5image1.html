I struggled with the patterns at first. I have done plenty of visual work over the years but aside from making a single gift for somebody I don't think I've done pattern work since I was a teenager. That is when it hit me. Why not just replicate the kind of pattern work I did when I was young. In the nineties I used to make texture pack mods for DOS and early Windows based games like Wolfenstein 3D and Doom, or I'd use Mario Paint or just native MS Paint and do tile editing for backgrounds or animations. So, I decided to build out a grid system to color pixels into patterns but leave conventional expectations of how pixel graphic bricklaying should look. The first pattern was an analogous color relationship just pushed into high contrast to seem completely unmatchable to real world structures that wouldnt be completely plastic. Intentionally leaning toward orginal NES vibes. The second is a split-complimentary and is intended more toward replicating Win 3.1 background tiling.
