This is 33 Thomson Street, technically in Tribeca, just below Chinatown. An enormous windowless monolith unlike anything around itself, or anything in the entire city, realistically. Constructed in the 1960s and 1970s as a telecommunications hub. It is now suspected to house a massive surveillance node for the National Security Agency’s partnership program with AT&T called FAIRVIEW. Bits from the Edward Snowden leaks and some subsequent investigative follow ups by Laura Poitras have confirmed as much. This building has been a conceptual and aesthetic inspiration in science-fiction film and video games for being a perfect example of that alien invasion from within, from underneath our reality, rather than from off somewhere in the cold of the universe. This building represents an early point of rupture between a world that was and a world that is in a state of becoming. From marketplace financial capital and telephony and the world of communication relays into circuitboards, virtualized networks, the cloud, and algorithmic surveillance. If you are on the team that believes God comes through artificial intelligence’s path to the singularity, this is exactly where you should kneel on the cement, fold your hands, direct your gaze upward, close your eyes, and ask for absolution.
